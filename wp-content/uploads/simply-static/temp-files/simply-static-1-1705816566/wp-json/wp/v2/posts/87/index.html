Ingredients<\/h3>\n\n\n\n
    \n
  • 150ml greek yogurt<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • \u00bd a clove garlic, crushed<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • finely chopped to make 1 tbsp dill<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 1 tbsp olive oil<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 3 tbsp salted butter<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • \u00bd tsp hot smoked paprika<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 2 eggs, (as fresh as possible)<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • a small handful flat-leaf parsley, chopped<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • 2 slices sourdough, griddled<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Method<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    STEP 1<\/strong>
    Put the greek yogurt in a large bowl and whisk in the garlic, dill, olive oil and some seasoning. Leave on the side while you prep the rest of the ingredients so the yogurt can lose the chill of the fridge.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    STEP 2<\/strong>
    Heat the butter in a small pan until melted, stir in the paprika and leave over a low heat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    STEP 3<\/strong>
    Poach the eggs by gently cracking into a pan of just-simmering water. Leave for 2-3 minutes then scoop out with a slotted spoon and gently poke the yolk to see if it is done to your liking. Drain on kitchen paper.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    STEP 4<\/strong>
    Divide the yogurt between 2 bowls. Add an egg to each then drizzle over the paprika butter and sprinkle with a little parsley. Serve with the sourdough\u00a0for\u00a0dunking.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
